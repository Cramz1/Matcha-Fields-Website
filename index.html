Our Story

A place to breathe,
slow down, and belong

Welcome to our guesthouse surrounded by the beautiful tea fields of Wazuka, Kyoto.

Wazuka is one of Japan's most renowned tea-producing regions, with more than 800 years of tea-growing history. Nearly half of Kyoto's famous Uji tea is cultivated here, creating a breathtaking landscape of endless green tea fields and peaceful countryside scenery.

Wake up to the sound of birds in the morning, stroll through the tea fields during the day, and relax beneath a quiet sky filled with stars at night. Our guesthouse offers a chance to step away from the rush of everyday life and reconnect with nature in a calm and authentic Japanese setting.

Popular local experiences include tea tastings, guided walks through the tea plantations, and bicycle tours around the countryside. We hope our guesthouse becomes a place where travelers can meet, share stories, and experience the beauty of Japanese culture and nature together.

Come and enjoy the peaceful atmosphere of Wazuka.

Hi, I'm Mao.

Host · Stay at Matcha Fields · Wazuka, Kyoto

I was born and raised by the beach in Chiba, Japan, and I've always loved traveling, meeting new people, and sharing cultures.

I run cozy guesthouses in Wazuka, Kyoto, and Tokyo. Each place has its own atmosphere — from peaceful tea fields to lively local neighborhoods — but my goal is always the same: to make guests feel relaxed, welcome, and connected to Japan in a personal way.

I enjoy sharing local recommendations, hidden spots, and everyday experiences with guests. Many parts of the houses include DIY touches, and I'm especially interested in creating warm, sustainable, and community-minded spaces.

I'd be very happy to welcome you and help you enjoy a more personal side of Japan.
